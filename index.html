Step 1: The White Cross

Form a cross on the white face by matching each white edge to its center.

Goal: Create a white cross by placing all 4 white edge pieces in the correct position.

How it works: The cross is intuitive - there are no algorithms to memorize! Find each white edge and move it to the top layer, then position it correctly.

Tips:

  • Match each white edge to its matching center color
  • The white cross should have white in the center and the side color matching its adjacent center
  • Practice until you can do this in under 5 seconds

Example: One cross move

Step 2: Solve White Corners

Place the white corners in their correct positions.

Goal: Get all white corners in the bottom layer with the white face down.

Algorithm: Use this simple algorithm when the white corner is in the wrong spot:

R U R' U'

Finding corners:

  • Look for a white corner in the top layer
  • If white is on the right: R U R' U'
  • If white is on the left: L' U' L U
White on Right
R U R' U'
White on Left
L' U' L U

Step 3: Middle Layer Edges

Solve the edges in the middle layer (between the white and yellow faces).

Goal: Complete the first two layers (F2L).

Steps:

  • Find an edge in the top layer that doesn't have yellow
  • Match it with its center color
  • Apply the algorithm to insert it
Goes Right
U R U' R' U' F' U F
Goes Left
U' L' U L U F U' F'

Step 4: Yellow Cross

Create a yellow cross on the top layer.

Algorithm: F R U R' U' F'

Apply this until you see a yellow cross:

  1. Yellow dot → Apply 3 times
  2. Yellow hook → Apply 2 times
  3. Yellow line → Apply 1 time
  4. Yellow cross → Done!

Step 5: Position Yellow Edges

Move the yellow edges to their correct positions.

Algorithm: R U R' U R U2 R'

Keep applying until edges are correctly positioned. The corners might get messed up - that's okay!

Adjacent
R U R' U R U2 R'
Opposite
Apply 2x

Step 6: Orient Yellow Corners

Make all yellow corners face up (the "headlights" or "fish" pattern).

Goal: Get all yellow stickers facing up on the top layer.

Main Algorithm: R' D' R D

How to use:

  1. Find a corner that doesn't have yellow facing up
  2. Hold it in the front-right-top position
  3. Repeat R' D' R D until that corner has yellow facing up
  4. Rotate the top layer to bring the next unsolved corner to front-right
  5. Repeat until all corners are solved!

Tip: Look for the "fish" pattern (one yellow corner facing up) - you're almost done!

Sune / Fish
R U R' U R U2 R'
Anti-Sune
R' U' R U' R' U2 R

Step 7: Permute Yellow Corners

Final step! Swap corners to their correct positions.

Algorithm: R' D' R D

Hold the cube so a corner that needs to move is in the front-right-top. Apply the algorithm until it's in the right spot. Rotate the top and repeat for other corners.

Congratulations! You've solved the cube!

F2L Lookahead Guide

Learn to plan your next F2L pair while executing the current one. This is the key to fast solve times!

What is Lookahead?

Lookahead means finding your next F2L pair before you finish the current one. Instead of stopping between pairs, you keep your eyes moving and plan ahead. The goal is to have zero pause between F2L pairs.

Visual Example

Watch this scramble and try to plan the first 2 F2L pairs:

Solution: R U R' (first pair), then U' R U R' (second pair)

Key Concepts
1. Slow Down

Start your solves slowly. It's better to solve at 80% speed with 0 pauses than 100% speed with 5 pauses. Speed will come naturally as lookahead improves.

2. Track the Pieces

While executing an algorithm, track where your next pair pieces are. Look at the opposite side of where you're turning - that's where your next pair usually is!

3. Look at the Last Layer

After each F2L pair, the corner and edge you're about to solve should be visible on the last layer. Scan the U layer while inserting your current pair.

4. Practice Inspection

During your 15-second inspection, find your first 2-3 F2L pairs. Plan the first pair, but also note where pairs 2 and 3 are. This builds lookahead habits.

Drills to Improve Lookahead
Slow Solve Drill
Solve at half speed. Your goal is ZERO pauses. If you pause, you're going too fast. Gradually increase speed as lookahead improves.
Pause Drill
Do a solve but STOP after every F2L pair. Find the next pair, then continue. This trains your brain to see pairs quickly.
Cross+1 Drill
Solve cross + first F2L pair with lookahead. Stop and find the next 3 pairs. This isolates lookahead practice to just the F2L stage.
Color Neutrality
Practice solving from different starting colors. This helps you see F2L pairs from all angles and improves overall recognition.
Common Lookahead Mistakes
  • Looking at your hands - You don't need to watch every turn! Trust your muscle memory.
  • Rushing - Going too fast prevents you from seeing the next pair. Slow down!
  • Not scanning the U layer - Most pairs are on the last layer. Always check there first.
  • Stopping between pairs - Even a 1-second pause kills your flow. Keep moving!
  • Only looking at one side - Pairs can be anywhere. Do a full 360° scan with your eyes.
Pro Tips

Start with easy pairs: Look for white corner + matching edge pairs first (Easy Inserts). Save the harder cases for later in your scan.

Use peripheral vision: You don't always need to look directly at a piece. Your peripheral vision can help you spot where pieces are.

Predict rotations: If you know you'll need a y or y' move to access the next pair, plan it into your current algorithm when possible.

Build muscle memory for recognition: The more you practice, the faster you'll recognize F2L cases instantly - this frees up brain power for lookahead.
